Example lesson

Lesson 1: introduce yourself clearly

Prompt “Tell me about yourself and the kind of role you are looking for.”

Answer in 30 seconds. Do not memorize a script. Use one structure: background, current skill, target role.

AI feedback preview Your answer should be clear, natural, and specific.
Clarity: one main point
Naturalness: not too scripted
Tone: confident but not salesy
Retry frame “My background is ___. Recently I worked on ___. Now I am looking for ___.”

One retry is enough for a first session. Save your before-and-after answer, then continue with tomorrow's scene.

FAQ

Questions before joining early access

Is this for beginners?

It can help beginners, but the strongest fit is someone who already knows some English and needs real work practice.

Is this only for speaking?

No. The plan includes speaking, email, chat, meetings, interviews, project updates, and client calls.

Will the AI correct grammar?

Grammar feedback can be useful, but the first priority is whether your answer is clear, natural, and appropriate for the work situation.

Can I use it before an interview?

Yes. The interview unit is designed for short answer practice, not memorizing long scripts.

Does this replace a human English teacher?

No. It is a practice tool for repeatable work scenes. A human teacher can still be valuable for deeper coaching.

How much time should I practice?

Start with 10 minutes a day: one prompt, one answer, one feedback pass, and one retry.

Can I save my recommended path?

Yes. Enter your email to save the path and receive the next practice lesson when it is ready.

What happens after I join?

You will get the recommended workplace English path and updates for the next practice lessons.
