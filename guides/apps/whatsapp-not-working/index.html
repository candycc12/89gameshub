Why Is WhatsApp Not Working? Common Fixes for Android and iPhone

If WhatsApp is not sending messages, connecting, verifying your number, or opening on WhatsApp Web, start with the simple checks before reinstalling or changing account settings.

Short answer

Most WhatsApp problems come from a weak connection, an outdated app, blocked permissions, low storage, a verification code delay, VPN interference, or a temporary service outage. First check Wi-Fi or mobile data, restart the phone, update WhatsApp from the official store, confirm permissions, and make sure your phone number and date settings are correct. Do not share verification codes or use unofficial code services.

Main Fixes for WhatsApp Problems

1. Check your connection and disable VPN temporarily

WhatsApp needs a stable internet connection. Switch from Wi-Fi to mobile data, or from mobile data to Wi-Fi. If you are using a VPN, proxy, school network, workplace network, or public Wi-Fi, test WhatsApp on a normal mobile connection.

2. Update WhatsApp from the official store

Outdated versions can break sending, calling, notifications, or verification. Update from Google Play on Android or the App Store on iPhone. Avoid APK download sites and unofficial app mirrors, even if they promise an older version.

3. Check phone permissions

If calls, photos, contacts, microphone, or notifications are not working, open your phone settings and review WhatsApp permissions. On Android, also check battery optimization and background data. On iPhone, check Settings, Notifications, Cellular Data, Photos, Microphone, and Contacts.

4. Free storage and restart the phone

Low storage can stop media downloads, backups, and updates. Clear unused files, remove large media from other apps, restart the phone, then reopen WhatsApp. If WhatsApp opens but media will not download, storage and connection are the first things to check.

5. Handle verification code problems carefully

Confirm the country code and phone number, wait before requesting another code, and check that your SIM can receive SMS or calls. Do not ask another person to receive your code. Do not enter a WhatsApp code into any third-party website or service.

6. Fix WhatsApp Web separately

WhatsApp Web depends on your browser, your linked-device session, and sometimes your phone connection. Refresh the page, update the browser, disable aggressive extensions, scan the QR code again, and unlink old devices you no longer use.

Safety Notes Before You Try Risky Fixes

Never share your WhatsApp verification code. Anyone asking for your code can use it to take over your account. WhatsApp support will not ask you to send a one-time code through chat, email, social media, or a random website.

Be careful with search results, social posts, and messages that claim to fix WhatsApp instantly. Do not install modified WhatsApp apps, unofficial APKs, cloned apps, or tools that promise to bypass verification. They can expose your messages, contacts, device data, or account access.

If you think your account is compromised, open official WhatsApp help, secure your phone number, review linked devices, and warn close contacts not to trust urgent money requests or strange messages from your account.

FAQ

Why is WhatsApp not sending messages?

Messages may fail because your internet connection is unstable, WhatsApp needs an update, the recipient is offline, your phone storage is low, or WhatsApp is having a temporary service issue.

Why is WhatsApp not connecting?

Check Wi-Fi or mobile data, disable VPN temporarily, restart the phone, update WhatsApp, and make sure your phone date and time are correct.

Why am I not receiving a verification code?

Verification codes can be delayed by weak mobile signal, wrong phone number format, SMS blocking, too many attempts, or carrier issues. Do not use third-party code services.

Is WhatsApp down right now?

If WhatsApp fails for many people at the same time, wait and check official WhatsApp channels or the Help Center. Avoid reinstalling repeatedly during a service outage.

Should I reinstall WhatsApp?

Try connection, update, permissions, storage, and restart steps first. Before reinstalling, confirm that your chats are backed up if you need to keep them.
